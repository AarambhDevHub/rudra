# Code of Conduct

## Our Pledge

Rudra is a project by Aarambh Dev Hub — a community built around learning, building, and growing together. We are committed to making participation in this project a welcoming, respectful, and harassment-free experience for everyone, regardless of age, background, experience level, nationality, gender, sexual orientation, disability, religion, or any other characteristic.

We pledge to act in ways that contribute to an open, inclusive, and healthy community.

---

## Our Standards

### Behavior we encourage

- Using welcoming and inclusive language
- Being respectful of differing viewpoints and experiences
- Giving and gracefully accepting constructive feedback
- Focusing on what is best for the community and the project
- Showing empathy toward other community members
- Helping newcomers — everyone starts somewhere
- Acknowledging mistakes and learning from them

### Behavior that is not acceptable

- Harassment, intimidation, or discrimination in any form
- Offensive, derogatory, or sexualized language or imagery
- Personal attacks or insults — criticize ideas, not people
- Trolling, deliberately disruptive behavior, or bad-faith arguments
- Publishing someone's private information without consent (doxxing)
- Sustained disruption of discussions, issues, or community events
- Any conduct that a reasonable person would consider inappropriate in a professional setting

---

## Scope

This Code of Conduct applies in all community spaces:

- GitHub repository (issues, pull requests, discussions, comments)
- Discord server ([discord.com/invite/HDth6PfCnp](https://discord.com/invite/HDth6PfCnp))
- YouTube comments on Aarambh Dev Hub videos
- Any other space where you represent Rudra or Aarambh Dev Hub

It also applies outside these spaces when someone's behavior toward community members could reasonably affect the community's health or safety.

---

## Enforcement

### Reporting

If you witness or experience unacceptable behavior, please report it to the project maintainers. Find contact information on the GitHub profile at [github.com/aarambh-darshan](https://github.com/aarambh-darshan).

All reports will be handled with discretion. The identity of the reporter will not be shared without explicit permission.

### What to include in a report

- Your account/contact information (optional but helpful for follow-up)
- Names or usernames of people involved
- Description of the incident — what happened, when, where
- Links to relevant content if it occurred online
- Any additional context you think is important

### Our commitment

- We will acknowledge all reports within **48 hours**
- We will review the situation fairly and confidentially
- We will not retaliate against anyone who reports in good faith
- We will keep the reporter informed of the outcome to the extent possible

---

## Consequences

Community leaders will determine consequences based on the nature, severity, and context of the violation. The following guidelines apply:

### 1. Correction

**Trigger:** Minor first offense — inappropriate language, minor disrespectful comment.

**Action:** A private written warning explaining the violation and what behavior is expected going forward.

---

### 2. Warning

**Trigger:** A single significant incident or repeated minor violations.

**Action:** A formal warning with specific consequences for continued behavior. A period of restricted interaction with community spaces may be imposed.

---

### 3. Temporary Ban

**Trigger:** Sustained inappropriate behavior, harassment, or any serious violation.

**Action:** Temporary ban from all community interaction — GitHub, Discord, and other spaces. Duration determined case by case. No public or private interaction with the people involved during this period.

---

### 4. Permanent Ban

**Trigger:** Sustained pattern of harmful behavior, harassment of an individual, or aggravated violations.

**Action:** Permanent removal from all community spaces with no path to reinstatement.

---

## Appeals

If you believe an enforcement action was taken in error, you may appeal by contacting the maintainers directly. Appeals are reviewed by at least one person not involved in the original decision.
